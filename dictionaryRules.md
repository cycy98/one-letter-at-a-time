# Dictionary Rules

1\. Words must be normalized.

1.1. Diacritical marks are removed (e.g. café becomes cafe).

1.2. Letters or characters from other scripts or Old English runes are not permitted (e.g. ß, þ or ƿ) and will be considered invalid except if there is a standard modern spelling.

1.3. Ligatures are considered two different letters (e.g. 'archæologist' becomes 'archaeologist'), and also can be added with the second letter omitted (e.g. 'archæologist' becomes 'archeologist').

1.4. Punctuation (except hyphens and apostrophes) is either removed (e.g. full stops for acronyms), replaced with a hyphen (e.g. em dashes) or the name (C++ → c-plus-plus).

1.5. Words, even proper nouns, are lowercased.

1.6. Repeated punctuation is collapsed.

1.7. Hyphens at the beginning of words are ignored.

2\. Word validity:

2.1. A word may be considered valid if it is listed in any dictionary. This excludes dictionaries with user-generated content, such as Wiktionary and Urban Dictionary, which fall under rule 2.4.

2.2. If a word contains a number in its name, such as '8-cell' or '1000th':

- If the word is a number itself, it may not be attested unless it is one word long or between 0 and 100.
- If it can be replaced by any arbitrary integer, it may not be attested. This excludes one-word-long numbers written in letters (such as 'thousandth'), as well as words related to mathematics complying with the scientific section (3.)
- If at least one of its definitions or usages complies with the above rules, it may be added in letters and hyphenated, e.g. '100-percent' (one-hunderd-percent) or '360-degree' three-hundred-degree, not as an angle but as a move.

2.3. If a word comes from user-generated content on a game, website, Fandom/modern encyclopedia, etc. (such as Wiktionary), it can only be added if the content is at least one-year-old and can be verified as such. Words from comments, social media messages, or blogs will not be added.

2.4. A fictional word in a game without UGC may be added if the game is popular enough. This excludes word games, where words cannot be added, and fictional chemistry.

2.5. The inflection (plural of a noun, conjugation of a verb, etc.) of a word or a matching form (adding a derivational suffix) doesn't require as much justification.

2.6 If a multiword can be abbreviated (e.g., by a blending or acronym), then both the abbreviation and the multiword can be added, with the multiword being hyphenated.

2.7. LLMs, or other generative AIs based on text, and lyrics are not considered sources for words.

3\. Scientific words

3.1. For chemistry-related words, it can only be added if real or already discovered, so ununenium won't be added.

3.2. Names of shapes beyond 10 dimensions are not allowed; for any shape to be added, it must either be common or is one of some finite category

3.3. Googolisms from the -illion system, such as million, billion, trillion, etc., can only be added if the numeral prefix before is less than one hundred (i.e., less than a millillion, 10^3003), but similar systems (-illard, Donald Knuth's -yllion) can be added under the same rule.
